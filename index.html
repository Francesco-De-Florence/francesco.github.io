About Me

Bridging Deep Learning
& Wireless Communication

I am Francesco De Florence, an electrical and electronic engineering researcher based at Islamic University, Kushtia, Bangladesh. My academic journey began in 2020 and has led me through a Bachelor's degree (CGPA 3.59/4.00) into an ongoing Master of Science program, where I work at the intersection of deep learning and modern wireless communication systems.

As a Research Assistant in the AI and Machine Learning Lab under Prof. Md. Humayun Kabir, I investigate challenging problems in 5G OFDM systems, channel estimation, WiFi-based sensing, and network security. My bachelor's thesis introduced a Conv1D–LSTM Autoencoder for PAPR mitigation in 5G OFDM, and my ongoing M.Sc. research targets real-time violence detection using WiFi Channel State Information (CSI) signals.

Beyond research, I am a passionate educator, a multi-level Physics Olympiad participant, a national award-winning flute player, and an active student leader. I aim to contribute to intelligent, next-generation communication infrastructure through rigorous applied research.
